to Anke
 

Yesterday (05.01.2016)

lying under my three blankets with my hands stuck deep into my armpits

(for safety's sake, you know)

and that unsuppressable always-ready-to-shoot-before-they-apologize Anke

(d'you happen to know her?)

with her goggled-out visual arguments for color-blind block-heads was still nudging me (alas! not under my blankets but in my thoughts).

Munching over and over our dispute on half-truths

(you might very easily forgot it long ago but I have warned you about my retarded thinking ways)

I found an analogy that drives the last nail into the coffin lid of half-truths.

Here it is.

You go to a supermarket, buy a jar of honey then open it and add a drop of petrol.

Is it a jar of honey now?

If 'yes' – enjoy it!

If 'no' – you deny there is a half-truth, a quarter-truth or any other-fractioned-truth whatsoever.

Period.

Then there popped up another question.

About truth application. Or truth manipulation.

In short, about the use of truth.

I am a bricklayer. True.

I am a mujik. True.

I am a dinosaur. True.

But when I combine these three truths and keep flagging-wagging them up so often they transform into my personal pea-cock tail and my mute prayer to the pagan Goddess: “please! Say it is the brightest and the biggest one you've ever seen!”

And this is a wholesome truth. A humiliating truth.

To restore my self-respect and so deep hurt male pride

(“she's just screwed me! That little b....!”)

I have only to chop wood (which I do not have because rely on electro-heating) or else clean the yard paths from the snow and they already look more like trenches than paths...

Good morning, Anke!

You presented me with warm glow in these black-outed winter months.

Who of us is more obliged, dear?

yours`

sehrguey
